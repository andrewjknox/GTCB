THE SHAPE OF THE PLAN
Vert and ToF aren't spread evenly — each week is built around one big mountain day (your Friday
keystone) plus a second hill day, with bike/row engine work and one strength session woven around them.
Peak weeks split into a long day plus a back-to-back (B2B) second day on tired legs. The numbers are
targets to aim at, not contracts — adjust to how the body (and Effer) say things are tracking.

Phase notes
What each block is really for, and how the strength and venues plug in.
Base · W1–8 (Jun–Jul)
Build the durability bank while running is restricted.
Job:   grow the aerobic engine (bike/row), bank vert by hiking, and condition the quads with downhill
running — all green-lit for your current status.
Strength: heaviest of the whole plan. Rotate T1→T2→T3; deloads use T4. This is where strength has
the most value, so don't skip it.
Venues: ease in locally (Tegg's, Lyme), build toward Edale/Bowden for the longer days. First B2B in
W7.
Gate:    W8 Effer check decides whether running reintroduction starts in W9.
Build · W9–16 (Aug–Sep)
Turn the base into race-specific mountain fitness.
Job:   reintroduce running (if cleared), blend run/hike, ramp vert and stack back-to-back weekends.
Night practice and course recon begin.
Strength: maintain, don't build — rotate T1–T3, but drop to T4 on the big B2B/recon weeks (W14–
15) so it doesn't add fatigue.
Venues: Edale becomes the keystone; Mam Tor for runnable reintro descents; Costa Blanca recon
(W14–15) for the long sustained descents the Peak can't give.
Peak · W17–19 (Oct)
The biggest, most race-like work — then start shedding fatigue.
Job:   peak vert (W17, ~4,000m) and peak time on feet (W18, the full dress rehearsal). W18
rehearses everything — kit, nutrition, night, pacing.
Strength: maintenance only (T4 → T5). Protect the mountain days; strength must not blunt them.
Venues: Edale night B2Bs in the multi-loop car-aided format; a final long rehearsal (Edale or Costa
Blanca).
Taper · W20–23 (Oct–Nov)
Arrive in Finestrat fresh, springy and uninjured.
Job:   cut volume progressively, keep a little sharpness, sleep and eat well. Resist the urge to "test"
fitness — the work is banked.
Strength: T5 maintenance only, fading to activation. Last real session ~7–10 days out.
Venues: local and low-fuss. W23 is travel + a short shakeout; plan a Friday-afternoon nap before
the 23:00 start.

Course-specific reminders & key dates
Where the race is won or lost
Puig Campana climb by ~km5, in the dark, on fresh legs — the discipline test. Go out far too easy.
Aitana (~1,558m, the roof) around km70 — caps a long high section; this is the crux of the day.
Late saw-tooth (Sella ~km80, climbs again to ~km95) — brutal on tired legs. Your peak B2Bs
rehearse exactly this: climbing when already wrecked.
Night-fresh running — the unusual demand of a 23:00 start. Practise it specifically from W13, plus the
Friday-afternoon nap.
Milestone calendar
W8 (27 Jul) — Effer gate: run reintroduction decision.
W9 (3 Aug) — running reintroduction begins, if cleared.
W13 (31 Aug) — night-running practice + fuelling rehearsal begins.
W14–15 (7–14 Sep) — Costa Blanca course recon.
W17 (28 Sep) — peak vert + long night B2B.
W18 (5 Oct) — full dress rehearsal.
W23 (Fri 13 Nov, 23:00) — race day, Finestrat.
THE REHAB LINE RUNS THROUGH EVERYTHING
The whole plan is gated on the left kinetic chain staying healthy. Descent reps and the run reintroduction
are the highest-risk elements — keep them inside what Effer signs off, watch how things settle over 24–
48h, and don't let a target number on this sheet override what the body is telling you. A finish line in
November beats a hero week in July.
