The near–far ladder
The trick is having an option at every distance, so the drive never becomes the reason a session doesn't
happen. Big vert days justify the longer trips; midweek and recovery vert stay local.
Keystone / night days — biggest vert & longest descents, worth the ~50 min: Edale, Bowden Bridge,
Mam Tor, Win Hill, Fairholmes.
Midweek — proper hills, less driving (~35–45 min): Tegg's Nose, Shutlingsloe.
Quick local — top-up vert when the Peak's too far (~20–30 min): Lyme Park, Werneth Low.
Specialist — for a specific stimulus: Bamford (concentrated steep), The Roaches (technical footing).

Keystone & night-session venues
Worth the longer drive — the big-vert and long-descent days, including your multi-loop night rehearsals.
Edale (Kinder Scout) 
KEYSTONE
~50 min / rail ~45 min
Your proven night-loop base — the biggest varied vert and the longest sustained runnable descents within
reach.
VERT / LAP
~500–600 m (Grindsbrook / Ringing Roger to the
Kinder edge)
DISTANCE / LAP
~7–10 km — your established 7–8 mi loop
FACILITIES
Pay car park, toilets, Penny Pot café, train station
opposite
TERRAIN & DESCENT
Mixed paths, some technical up high; long, sustained
descents
NOTES
Multiple cloughs from one car park = vary the loop without moving the car. Car-as-aid-station already dialled.
Machines: cash/app, weak signal — sort payment first. Or take the train and skip parking entirely. Best venue
for the W13–18 night rehearsals.
Bowden Bridge, Hayfield (Kinder west) 
KEYSTONE
~40 min
Closest serious climbing — Kinder from the west, with contained climbs you can rep back to the car.
VERT / LAP
~300–500 m (William Clough / toward Kinder
downfall)
DISTANCE / LAP
~8–10 km full; shorter reservoir reps possible
FACILITIES
Pay car park (free before 9am), toilets across the road
TERRAIN & DESCENT
Good trail, runnable; reservoir track for easy transit
NOTES
10 min closer than Edale with comparable vert — strong big-day alternative. Cash/RingGo only, no signal at
the car park; pay before you lose signal or arrive before 9am. Fills fast on weekends.
Mam Tor / Great Ridge 
KEYSTONE
~50 min
The lap machine — beautifully runnable grassy descents on an endlessly repeatable ridge.
VERT / LAP
~250–300 m per Mam Tor rep; more along the full
ridge
DISTANCE / LAP
~5 km; extend via Hollins Cross / Lose Hill
FACILITIES
Castleton car park (toilets, café) or Mam Nick (higher
start)
TERRAIN & DESCENT
Grassy, runnable, smooth — ideal descent-rep ground
NOTES
Best venue for racking descent reps specifically. Very busy on sunny weekends — go early or midweek. Start
at Mam Nick to save the first climb if banking pure descent volume.

Win Hill (Heatherdene) 
KEYSTONE
~50–55 min
Clean, quiet single-hill reps — steep up, fast runnable down, straight back to the car.
VERT / LAP
~300 m, steep and direct
DISTANCE / LAP
~6–7 km round trip
FACILITIES
Heatherdene pay car park, good toilets
TERRAIN & DESCENT
Steep, mostly good footing; a proper runnable
descent
NOTES
Quieter than Mam Tor/Edale. Note the road crossing from the car park. A no-nonsense vert/descent rep
session.
Fairholmes (Derwent) 
KEYSTONE
~55 min
The aid-station-style base — café and toilets at the car for long, car-supported vert days.
VERT / LAP
~250–300 m per climb to Derwent Edge
DISTANCE / LAP
~6–8 km
FACILITIES
Large car park, toilets, café, cycle hire
TERRAIN & DESCENT
Reservoir tracks + moorland edge climbs; mixed
footing
NOTES
Closest match to your Edale car-as-aid-station setup, with the bonus of a real café. Good for stacking several
climbs between reservoir-level returns.

Midweek venues
Proper hills with far less driving — your regular second hill day when Edale is too much faff.
Tegg's Nose Country Park 
MIDWEEK
~35 min
Best drive-to-vert ratio near you — a country park built for short steep laps.
VERT / LAP
~120–180 m, steep and repeatable
DISTANCE / LAP
~3–4 km
FACILITIES
Dedicated car park, toilets, café
TERRAIN & DESCENT
Managed paths, some steps; short sharp descents
NOTES
Repping within ~35 min of leaving home. Lower vert per lap, so add laps to hit the target. Can link to
Shutlingsloe for a bigger day. Ideal regular weekday session.
Shutlingsloe (Standing Stone) 
MIDWEEK
~40–45 min
Cheshire's "Matterhorn" — a sharp little peak with steep descents both sides, and quiet.
VERT / LAP
~200–250 m (more from Trentabank)
DISTANCE / LAP
~4–5 km
FACILITIES
Free car park (Standing Stone); Trentabank nearby
TERRAIN & DESCENT
Paved-stone path + steep steps; both descents steep
NOTES
Free parking, no time limits, much quieter than the Dark Peak honeypots. Steep descents make it good
eccentric work in a small package.
Quick local venues
For the "no time today" vert top-up — close enough that a short session is always worth it.
Lyme Park (Disley) 
LOCAL
~30 min
Gentle, grassy, lappable — lower descent load, good for recovery vert or grim-weather days.
VERT / LAP
~100–150 m (The Cage / Knott)
DISTANCE / LAP
~4–5 km
FACILITIES
NT car park (paid; free for members), toilets, café
TERRAIN & DESCENT
Grass & estate tracks; gentle, low-impact descents
NOTES
Gates ~8:30am–8pm, so not for very early/night sessions. Lower descent load makes it a good recovery-vert
or easy-day choice — and the deer are a bonus.

Specialist venues
Reach for these when you want one specific thing the others don't give.
Bamford Edge / Bamford Clough 
SPECIALIST
~50–55 min
Concentrated brutal steep — Bamford Clough is reckoned the steepest road in the UK.
VERT / LAP
~180–220 m over a very short distance
DISTANCE / LAP
~2–4 km (very steep)
FACILITIES
Roadside parking only — limited, arrive early
TERRAIN & DESCENT
Steep lane + gritstone edge; short descents
NOTES
Use it for power-hike strength and concentrated vert in little time. Not for long runnable descents — that's not
its job.
The Roaches (Gradbach) 
SPECIALIST
~50–55 min
Rugged gritstone — for technical footing and terrain skills the smoother hills don't build.
VERT / LAP
~250–300 m
DISTANCE / LAP
~6 km
FACILITIES
Free car park (Gradbach); no toilets
TERRAIN & DESCENT
Rocky, technical, uneven; shorter rougher descents
NOTES
Closest thing locally to GTCB's rough, ankle-testing footing. Great for foot-placement and stability work;
descents are technical rather than long.

Putting it together
Friday keystone day: Edale or Bowden Bridge for big vert; Mam Tor when descent reps are the focus;
Fairholmes for a café-supported long day.
Second / midweek hill day: Tegg's Nose as the default (least driving), Shutlingsloe for a steeper hit.
Recovery or squeezed days: Werneth Low or Lyme Park — local, low fuss, lower load.
Night rehearsals (W13–18): Edale, building from one loop to the full multi-loop, car-aided format
you've used before.
Specialist top-ups: Bamford for concentrated steep; The Roaches for technical footing.
WATCH THE DESCENT LOAD ON LAP DAYS
Stacking descents repeatedly from a fixed base ramps eccentric / left-chain load faster than point-to-point
routes do. Build rep counts up gradually, favour transit/flat additions over extra descent reps early on, and
see how the calf and knee settle over the following 24–48h before pushing the count. Keep this inside what
Effer's happy with. 
THE GAP THESE CAN'T CLOSE
Even Edale's descents are short next to the GTCB's 600–800m sustained drops. These venues build vert
and eccentric volume; the long unbroken descents are what your Spain trips and the September recon
weeks are for. Train the engine and the legs here, rehearse the real thing there.
